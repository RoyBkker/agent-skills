# Sources for avoid-ai-writing-nl (Dutch)

Created: 2026-07-17. Research method per jurigis/avoid-ai-writing-multilingual CLAUDE.md: web research conducted **in Dutch**, across four independent source layers (Wikipedia community, SEO/content blogs, academic, practitioner reports). No pattern was translated from the English original; universal patterns are marked as such.

Evidence labels used below and in SKILL.md:
- **statistical** — corpus study or measured frequencies
- **editorial-consensus** — ≥3 independent Dutch practitioner/editorial sources make the same claim
- **editorial / anecdotal** — fewer than 3 sources, or single-practitioner observation

## Layer 1 — Academic (statistical backbone)

### 1.1 Boer & Spoelstra (2026) — *the* Dutch corpus anchor
**Title:** Linguistic differences between self-written and generative AI-assisted student texts in Dutch bachelor CS education
**URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10639-026-13939-9 (Education and Information Technologies)
**Institutions:** Open Universiteit / Saxion. **Corpus:** 65 Dutch student texts (~300 words each).
**Findings used:** AI-assisted Dutch texts show **more evaluative/positive epithets of the type *cruciaal / krachtig / waardevol*** (medium effect, p<.05); **fewer personal pronouns** (large effect); lower perplexity; lower readability; longer texts. Discriminant analysis 94% accurate but 19% false positives on self-written texts; human teachers only 76%.
**Supports:** Tier-1 class justification (evaluative epithets), pattern #5 (fewer personal pronouns → no personal experience), the "signals, not proof" disclaimer.
**Limitation:** small corpus, one genre (student essays).

### 1.2 CLIN33 Shared Task (2024)
**Title:** The CLIN33 Shared Task on the Detection of Text Generated by Large Language Models
**URL:** https://clinjournal.org/clinj/article/view/182 (CLIN Journal 13, pp. 233–259); data DOI 10.5281/zenodo.10732813
**Institutions:** UAntwerpen, KU Leuven, Utrecht, Radboud, Elsevier a.o. Dutch + English, 6 genres.
**Findings used:** Dutch detection measurably harder than English (best macro-accuracy 0.75 vs 0.85). Statistically significant (p<.01), cross-lingual: LLM text has **less past tense, fewer numerals and proper nouns, less punctuation** (except more quotation marks). English readability heuristics do **not** transfer to Dutch.
**Supports:** patterns #6, #24; the disclaimer; the language-specific-research principle itself.

### 1.3 Universal statistical studies (English corpora — marked "universeel" in SKILL.md)
- **Kobak et al. 2024/2025**, *Delving into LLM-assisted writing in biomedical publications through excess vocabulary* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.07016 / Science Advances 11. Excess-word method; "delves" r=28.0, "crucial" δ=0.037; 454 excess style words in 2024. Supports the tier-vocabulary *method*; no Dutch replication exists.
- **Juzek & Ward 2025 (COLING)**, *Why Does ChatGPT "Delve" So Much?* — https://aclanthology.org/2025.coling-main.426/. 21 focal overused words; RLHF as likely cause.
- **Liang et al. 2024 (ICML)**, *Monitoring AI-Modified Content at Scale* — https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.07183. "commendable" 9.8×, "meticulous" 34.7×, "intricate" 11.2×.
- **Reinhart et al. 2025 (PNAS)** — https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.2422455122. Instruction-tuned LLMs: participial clauses at 2–5× human rate; nominalization-heavy style. Supports #26 (labeled universal).
- **Desaire et al. 2023 (Cell Rep. Phys. Sci.)** — https://www.cell.com/cell-reports-physical-science/fulltext/S2666-3864(23)00200-X. **SD of sentence length within a paragraph** discriminates (mean length does not); humans use more *but/however/although* and question marks. Supports #32.
- **Liang et al. 2023 (Patterns)** — https://www.cell.com/patterns/fulltext/S2666-3899(23)00130-7. Detector false-positive rate 61.3% on TOEFL essays. Supports the disclaimer.
- **MULTITuDE (EMNLP 2023)** https://aclanthology.org/2023.emnlp-main.616/ and **MultiSocial (ACL 2025)** https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.36/ — multilingual MGT benchmarks incl. Dutch; feasibility context only, no word-level Dutch findings.

### 1.4 Ghost references in Dutch science
**Source:** De Groene Amsterdammer / Data School Utrecht investigation, reported via Vox (Radboud): https://www.voxweb.nl/nieuws/steeds-vaker-spookreferenties-in-wetenschappelijke-artikelen-uit-nederland-door-ai-gebruik (2026). 136,000+ articles: fabricated references rose from 1/1,400 (2023) to 1/200 (2026).
**Supports:** #45 (spookbronnen) — statistical, artifact-level.

## Gaps and honest limitations

1. **No Dutch excess-vocabulary corpus study exists** (Kobak-style). Every individual Tier-1 word except the evaluative-epithet *class* (Boer & Spoelstra) rests on editorial consensus, not measured frequency. If such a study appears, re-verify the Tier-1 table against it.
2. **nl.wikipedia has no localized Signs-of-AI-writing page** — the community layer is thread archaeology, not a maintained guide.
3. **Onze Taal magazine content** (Aaron Mirck column 01-2025) is members-only and could not be read; the institutional claim used is from the freely accessible Taalloket page only.
4. **Major newspaper coverage** (NRC/Volkskrant/Trouw/De Standaard) not directly retrievable (paywalls); practitioner layer leans on trade platforms (Frankwatching, Villamedia) instead.
5. **Copula-avoidance (#26) and synonym chains (#27)** are documented for LLM output generally, not for Dutch specifically — labeled "universeel" in the skill.
6. **Je/u-drift as a tell** is practice observation from this project's own baseline testing, explicitly labeled "praktijkobservatie" in the skill; no published source found.
7. **Genus/congruentie-drift** ("onze team… ze") is observed in authentic generated Dutch (this project's test corpus of one) and folded under the translated-English family (#15) rather than claimed as an independent sourced pattern.
8. **Flanders evidence is thinner than Netherlands evidence** (3 nl-BE sources vs 20+ nl-NL); the Vlaanderen-notitie is calibrated accordingly (short, register-level only).
9. **Tells decay.** The em-dash weakening (Nov 2025) happened *during* the citable window of this research; expect the same for other patterns. Re-verify convergence counts before any 2.x release.

## Recommendations for future versions

1. Watch for a Dutch replication of the excess-vocabulary method (CLIN would be the venue) — it would let Tier-1 move from editorial-consensus to statistical.
2. The Frankwatching/SoMC lists skew marketing; a journalism-side list (Villamedia, RvdJ) would balance the genre coverage.
3. Track LinkedIn's own AI-slop detection announcements — platform-level suppression changes what slop survives in the wild, and therefore what the skill should prioritize.
